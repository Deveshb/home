Frequently Asked Questions

What if I exceed my paid plan limits?

Because we use 95th percentile billing, you won't be charged for your overages 5% of the time (about 1.5 days each month). If you exceed your limits for more than 5% of the month, the following overage fees will be added to the base price of your monthly bill:

Starter Plan: $0.25 each additional connection. $2.50 each additional GB of Data Transfer.

Apps at Scale Plan: $0.25 each additional connection. $2.50 each additional GB of Data Transfer.

Can I see my detailed metrics?

Yes. We're building a custom dashboard which will be rolled out very soon, but for now - email us and we'll send you a report. We'll even email you if you're about to exceed your plan.

Can I upgrade, downgrade, and cancel?

Yes. You can change your CloudBoost App size or even cancel at any time from your account panel. Note that we don't provide prorated refunds for downgrades or cancellations.

What kind of support will I receive?

All paid CloudBoost Accounts come with email and phone support from CloudBoost engineers. Additional levels of support, including 24x7 phone support, are available to Enterprise customers.

What is a concurrent connection?

A connection is a measure of the number of users that are using your app or site simultaneously. It's any open network connection to our servers. This isn't the same as the total number of visitors to your site or the total number of users of your app. In our experience, 1 concurrent corresponds to roughly 1,400 monthly visits. Our Starter Plan has a hard limit on the number of connections. All of the paid CloudBoost apps, however, are “burstable”, which means there are no hard caps on usage. REST API requests don't count towards your connection limits.

What is the limit on the FREE Plan?

The free plan has a hard cap at 50 concurrent connections. When the 51st user tries to connect, the API will throw an error and any additional connections will fail. Our paid plans have no hard caps on usage.

What's the role of AI?

Our AI engine analyses how your app queries your data and according to that move your data to a data-store that suits well for your app - all of this is done to make quering crazy fast.
